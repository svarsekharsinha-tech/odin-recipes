Easy Lasagna Recipe

Description

Easy Lasagna strips away the labor-intensive steps of the traditional Italian classic without sacrificing the comforting, hearty flavors that make it a family favorite. By utilizing conveniences like no-boil noodle sheets and high-quality jarred marinara sauce, the preparation time is drastically reduced, transforming a typically complex weekend project into a manageable weeknight meal. The foundation remains a delicious stack of savory layers: seasoned ground beef or Italian sausage browned with aromatics, alternating with tender pasta and a rich, creamy blend of ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheese.

Once assembled, the oven does the heavy lifting, melding the distinct ingredients into a cohesive, bubbling masterpiece. As it bakes, the pasta sheets absorb the moisture from the sauce, becoming perfectly tender while the cheese on top broils to a golden, irresistible crisp. The result is a warm, gooey square of comfort that tastes like it simmered all day, offering a perfect balance of tangy tomato acidity and milky, melted cheese. It serves as an excellent centerpiece for a dinner gathering and often tastes even better the next day, making it a practical choice for meal prepping.

Ingredients

Cheese Filling

  • 15 oz. ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese
  • 3/4 cup Parmesan Sauce
  • 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper

Meat Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 3/4 lb ground beef
  • 3/4 lb ground Italian sausage
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 40 oz. marinara sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce

Lasagna Noodles/Cheese Topping

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 2.5 cups mozarella cheese

Instructions

Prep Work

  1. 1.Combine the cheese filling ingredients in a medium bowl and set aside. Measure out remaining ingredients.

Make The Meat Sauce

  1. 1.Heat olive oil over medium heat and add the diced onions. Let them soften for 10 minutes. (This allows them to release a little sugar which is a nice offset to the acidity of the tomatoes.)
  2. 2.Add the ground beef and sausage and increase heat to medium-high. Use a spatula to break up the meat so that it’s very fine and crumbled. Cook for 8-10 minutes, or until cooked through. Add garlic during the last minute. Drain excess grease.
  3. 3.Add the chicken broth and use a silicone spatula to “clean” the bottom and sides of the pot. Add the marinara sauce, tomato paste, hot sauce, and Worcestershire sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover partially and let the sauce simmer gently while you boil the pasta noodles.

Preheat Oven and Cook the Lasagna Noodles

  1. 1.Preheat oven to 375°.
  2. 2.Begin boiling a large pot of salted pasta water for the lasagna noodles. Once a rapid boil is reached, cook the noodles to al dente according to package instructions. (Set a timer to ensure you don’t overcook them.) Gently stir with a wooden spoon throughout cooking to prevent the noodles from sticking. Drain and rinse with cold water until noodles are completely cool.
  3. 4.Lay the cooled noodles flat on wax or parchment paper while you begin assembling the lasagna.

Assemble

  1. 1.Spread 1 heaping cup of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9 x 13-inch casserole dish. Next, add 4 lasagna noodles, overlapping them if needed.
  2. 2.Spread 1/3 of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles, followed by about 1 ½ cups of meat sauce.
  3. 3.Top with 4 more lasagna noodles, 1/3 of the ricotta cheese mixture, 1 ½ cups meat sauce.
  4. 4.Finish with 4 more lasagna noodles, the rest of the ricotta mixture, and the rest of the meat sauce.
  5. 5.Top with 2 ½ cups of Mozzarella cheese.
  6. 6.Spray foil with nonstick cooking spray and place it spray-side-down on top of the baking dish to prevent the cheese from sticking to it.

Bake

  1. 1.Bake for 35 minutes. Remove cover and bake for 10 more minutes
  2. 2.Remove and garnish with fresh parsley. Let it sit for 15 minutes prior to serving to allow the layers to set. Serve with garlic bread with cheese.
