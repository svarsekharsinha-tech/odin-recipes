Spicy Puffed Rice Recipe

Description

Spicy Puffed Rice, most commonly known in Assam and West Bengal as Jhal Muri, is an iconic "no-cook" street food snack.

Here is a quick breakdown of what makes it special:

The Base: It uses crispy puffed rice (murmura) which is very light and airy.

The Flavor: It is a bold explosion of tastesâ€”spicy from green chilies, tangy from lime juice, and savory from black salt.

The Secret Kick: The defining characteristic of authentic Jhal Muri is the splash of raw mustard oil, which adds a sharp, pungent zest that separates it from the sweeter "Bhel Puri" found in other parts of India.

Experience: It is traditionally mixed fresh in front of you and served in a newspaper cone (thonga), making it the ultimate cheap and satisfying evening snack.

Ingredients

  • Puffed Rice (Murmura): 2 cups
  • Onion: 1 small (finely chopped)
  • Green Chili: 1 or 2 (finely chopped) - adjust for spice tolerance!
  • Tomato: 1 small (chopped)
  • Mixture/Bhujia/Sev: A handful (any packet of crunchy salty snack like Aloo Bhujia)
  • Mustard Oil: 1 teaspoon (This is the secret ingredient for that authentic "Jhal Muri" kick)
  • Salt: To taste (Black salt is better if you have it)
  • Lemon: Half a lemon
  • Optional: Boiled potato (cubed), cucumber (chopped), or roasted peanuts

Steps

  1. Chop Veggies: Finely chop the onions, green chilies, and tomatoes.
  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In your bowl, mix the chopped onions, chilies, tomatoes, salt, and mustard oil. Squishing the onions and oil together slightly with a spoon (or clean hand) releases the flavor.
  3. Add the Crunch: Add the puffed rice (murmura) and the bhujia/mixture to the bowl.
  4. Toss: Squeeze the lemon juice over the top and mix everything immediately and thoroughly.
  5. Serve: Eat it right away! If you wait too long, the puffed rice will get soggy from the tomato and lemon juice.
