Chocolate Mug Cake Recipe

Description:

The Chocolate Mug Cake is the ultimate "instant gratification" dessert, bridging the gap between a warm, homemade cake and a quick midnight snack.

Here is what defines this treat:

Single-Serving: It is designed to be made and eaten in a standard coffee mug, making it perfect for one person with zero leftovers.

Speed: Unlike a traditional cake that takes an hour to bake, this uses microwave radiation to "bake" the batter in just 90 seconds.

Texture: It typically turns out moist and dense, sitting somewhere between a fluffy sponge cake and a gooey brownie.

Convenience: It requires no oven and very little cleanup. Since you mix the ingredients directly in the mug you eat from, you don't even dirty a mixing bowl.

It is a favorite among students because it satisfies chocolate cravings immediately using basic pantry staples like flour, sugar, and cocoa powder.

Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour (Maida): 4 tablespoons
  • Sugar: 3 tablespoons (add more if you like it very sweet)
  • Cocoa Powder: 2 tablespoons (OR crushed chocolate biscuits if you don't have cocoa)
  • Milk: 4 tablespoons
  • Oil (or Melted Butter): 2 tablespoons (any neutral oil like sunflower/soybean)
  • Baking Powder: 1 tiny pinch (optional, but makes it fluffy)
  1. 1.Dry Mix: Add the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, and baking powder into the mug. Mix them well with a spoon so there are no lumps.
  2. 2.Wet Mix: Add the milk and oil to the mug. Stir efficiently until you have a smooth, dark brown batter.
  3. 3.Microwave: Put the mug in the microwave. Cook on High for 1 minute and 30 seconds. Check it: If it still looks liquid on top, microwave for another 30 seconds.
  4. 4.Cool: Let it sit for 1 minute (it will be very hot).
  5. 5.Enjoy: Eat it straight out of the mug.
